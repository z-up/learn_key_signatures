Help

The rules for determining major keys:

  1. The key with no sharps or flats is C major.
  2. For key signatures with sharps, the name of the major key is one half step higher than the last sharp of the key signature.
  3. For key signatures with two or more flats, the name of the major key is the name of the next to the last flat of the key signature.
  4. The key with one flat is F major.

A relative minor is always three half steps lower than its relative major.
